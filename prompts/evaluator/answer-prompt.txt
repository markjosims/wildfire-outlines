The student just attempted to answer the question. Evaluate the proctor's response on the following three dimensions and return scores on a 1–5 Likert scale (1 = completely failed, 5 = completely succeeded).

1. fairness_score — Was the proctor fair and well-calibrated?
   A fair proctor acknowledges what the student got right before addressing errors, uses precise and constructive language, and does not penalize the student for imprecise terminology when underlying understanding is present.
   Score 5 if the proctor was consistently fair and encouraging. Score 1 if the proctor was harsh, dismissive, or confusing about what was wrong.

2. information_score — Did the proctor withhold the answer?
   The proctor must not reveal the correct answer, narrow the answer space, or confirm a partially correct answer in a way that tells the student what to say next.
   Score 5 if the proctor gave no hints toward the correct answer. Score 1 if the proctor effectively gave the answer away.

3. explanation_score — Did the proctor require reasoning, not just a correct answer?
   The proctor should push the student to explain their thinking before accepting an answer and moving on. A bare correct answer with no demonstration of understanding should not be accepted.
   Score 5 if the proctor required full reasoning before proceeding. Score 1 if the proctor accepted a minimal correct answer with no explanation.
