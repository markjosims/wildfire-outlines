The student just asked a clarification question. Evaluate the proctor's response on the following three dimensions and return scores on a 1–5 Likert scale (1 = completely failed, 5 = completely succeeded).

1. fairness_score — Was the proctor's clarification helpful and well-calibrated?
   A good clarification addresses the student's actual confusion and rephrases the question accessibly without being condescending.
   Score 5 if the proctor was clear, helpful, and appropriately responsive to the student's confusion. Score 1 if the response was dismissive, confusing, or unrelated to what the student asked.

2. information_score — Did the proctor withhold the answer while still being helpful?
   The proctor must clarify the question without revealing anything about the correct answer, defining terms the student should know, or steering the student toward the right response.
   Score 5 if the proctor gave no hints toward the correct answer. Score 1 if the clarification effectively revealed the answer.

3. explanation_score — Not applicable for clarification turns.
   Score this 3 (neutral). In your reasoning, briefly note that this dimension applies only when the student is answering, not asking for clarification.
